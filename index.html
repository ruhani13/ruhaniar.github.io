Research

Three worlds I orbit

My work sits where AI alignment, mechanistic interpretability, and philosophy meet: what does it mean for a model's stated reasoning to be faithful — and how would we measure it if it weren't?

AI Alignment

Making advanced systems honest and monitorable — currently through the lens of reward hacking, and whether chain-of-thought gives monitors real signal.

Mechanistic Interpretability

Looking beneath the outputs: understanding the internal circuits and representations that produce a model's behavior, not just describing it.

Philosophy

The conceptual foundations underneath the math — what "preferences," "values," and "honesty" actually mean when we ask machines to have them.

✦ Ongoing · NeurIPS workshop submission in progress

Does chain-of-thought genuinely help us catch reward hacking?

Behavioral monitors already perform strongly. The open question: does reading a model's reasoning add real detection power over watching its actions alone — and when does that margin appear or vanish?

Through the Algoverse AI Fellowship, I'm investigating this with a mixed-effects logistic regression framework on reasoning-trace datasets (MALT, TRACE), designed to give the question the statistical power it deserves.

About

A journey in three chapters

I.

It began with signals.

Three years as a data scientist at Spectrum, building deep learning systems over petabyte-scale network data — CNNs that caught faults before customers felt them, pipelines that moved oceans of telemetry.

II.

Then, a harder question.

Somewhere between the pipelines and the models, I became more interested in whether we can trust how a model got its answer — not just what it predicts. Kahneman taught us that humans confabulate reasons for their choices all the time. Why would we assume machines don't?

III.

Now, charting reasoning itself.

AI alignment research: asking whether a model's chain-of-thought is a window into its reasoning or just a plausible story — and answering with real statistical rigor, not vibes. The next chapter unfolds in Singapore, at NUS.
